# Swales 1990

## Bibliographic Info
- **Authors:** John M. Swales
- **Year:** 1990 (revised 2004)
- **Title:** Genre Analysis: English in Academic and Research Settings
- **Publisher:** Cambridge University Press
- **ISBN:** 978-0-521-33813-4
- **Revised:** Research Genres: Explorations and Applications (2004), Cambridge University Press

## Summary
Defines the CARS (Create A Research Space) model: a three-move rhetorical structure for research article introductions. Move 1: Establishing a Territory (claiming centrality, reviewing prior research). Move 2: Establishing a Niche (indicating a gap, counter-claiming). Move 3: Occupying the Niche (announcing research, outlining findings).

## Key Findings
- Atomic unit: the "rhetorical move" -- a functional text unit defined by communicative purpose, not form
- Move 2 (gap establishment) is most commonly missing in weak introductions
- The 2004 revision simplified Move 1 and expanded Move 3 to seven steps
- Moves are genre conventions, not argument quality measures
- Validated across disciplines through corpus studies
- ML classifiers (SVM, BERT, GPT-2) have been applied to automatic move detection

## Relevance to DR-004
Provides section-level structure verification, especially for introductions. Complements argument-level models (Toulmin, Walton). However, CARS was designed for empirical research introductions -- perspective papers and theoretical papers may not follow the gap-fill logic. For non-empirical papers, adapted move structures would be needed.
