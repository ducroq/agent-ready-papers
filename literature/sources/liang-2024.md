# Liang et al. 2024

## Bibliographic Info
- **Authors:** Weixin Liang, Yuhui Zhang, Hancheng Cao, Binglu Wang, Daisy Yi Ding, Xinyu Yang, Kailas Vodrahalli, Siyu He, Daniel Scott Smith, Yian Yin, Daniel A. McFarland, James Zou
- **Year:** 2024
- **Title:** Can Large Language Models Provide Useful Feedback on Research Papers? A Large-Scale Empirical Analysis
- **Venue:** NEJM AI, 1(8)
- **DOI:** 10.1056/AIoa2400196

## Summary
Compared GPT-4 feedback with human peer reviewer feedback from 15 Nature family journals (3,096 papers) and ICLR (1,709 papers). Found that GPT-4/human overlap (30.85% Nature, 39.23% ICLR) is comparable to human/human overlap (28.58% Nature, 35.25% ICLR). 57.4% of researchers found GPT-4 feedback helpful.

## Key Findings
- LLM feedback is "useful but lacking depth, especially in detailed critique"
- LLMs catch surface issues but struggle with deep argument analysis
- GPT-4/human agreement is comparable to human/human agreement (both ~30-39%)
- Validates using LLMs as a pre-flight check (not a replacement for peer review)

## Relevance to DR-004
Establishes that LLM-based paper review is viable but needs infrastructure. LLMs alone match human reviewers on surface feedback but miss deeper argument issues. This supports the project's core thesis: verification infrastructure (templates, registries, checklists) + LLM capability > LLM capability alone.
