# Page et al. 2021 (PRISMA 2020)

## Bibliographic Info
- **Authors:** Matthew J. Page, Joanne E. McKenzie, Patrick M. Bossuyt, et al.
- **Year:** 2021
- **Title:** The PRISMA 2020 statement: an updated guideline for reporting systematic reviews
- **Venue:** BMJ, 372, n71; also Systematic Reviews, 10, 89
- **DOI:** 10.1136/bmj.n71

## Summary
Updated 27-item checklist and flow diagram for reporting systematic reviews and meta-analyses. The gold standard for review paper reporting. Covers title, abstract, introduction, methods (eligibility, sources, search, selection, data extraction, quality assessment, synthesis), results, discussion, and other information.

## Key Findings
- 27-item checklist with specific reporting requirements per item
- Covers the full review pipeline from protocol to synthesis
- Widely adopted across disciplines (not just medicine)
- Demonstrates that structured checklists can be operationalized for paper verification
- Part of EQUATOR Network (~500 reporting guidelines, almost all for empirical health research)

## Relevance to DR-004
PRISMA shows what a fully developed verification checklist looks like for one paper type. The critical finding from Agent 3's research: EQUATOR has ~500 guidelines for empirical papers but essentially ZERO for theoretical, design science, perspective, or methodological papers. This gap is where the project could make its most distinctive contribution -- creating verification infrastructure for paper types that have no PRISMA-equivalent.
