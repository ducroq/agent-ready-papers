# van Eemeren & Grootendorst 2004

## Bibliographic Info
- **Authors:** Frans H. van Eemeren, Rob Grootendorst
- **Year:** 2004 (foundations in 1992)
- **Title:** A Systematic Theory of Argumentation: The Pragma-Dialectical Approach
- **Publisher:** Cambridge University Press
- **ISBN:** 978-0-521-53772-8
- **URL:** https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/systematic-theory-of-argumentation/C3A069AB7E067D451BE58744A444B570

## Summary
Models argumentation as a dialogical process between parties resolving a difference of opinion. Defines ten rules of critical discussion; systematic violations constitute fallacies. Four discussion stages: confrontation, opening, argumentation, concluding.

## Key Findings
- Ten normative rules provide a fallacy-detection checklist (each fallacy = rule violation)
- Burden of proof rule (Rule 2) maps directly to claim registry concept
- Strongest normative framework -- provides clear right/wrong judgments
- Requires "reconstruction" to apply to monologic text (papers vs dialogues)
- More suited to debate analysis than single-author paper assessment

## Relevance to DR-004
The ten rules could serve as a checklist for argument quality, but the dialogical reconstruction required makes it harder to operationalize than Toulmin or Walton for paper verification. Best used selectively: Rules 3 (straw man), 4 (relevance), 6 (begging the question), and 8 (logical validity) are directly applicable to checking academic arguments.
