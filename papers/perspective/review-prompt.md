## Instructions

Review the manuscript section(s) pasted below for submission readiness. Evaluate along these dimensions:

### 1. Rigor

<!-- Non-empirical paper — use Variant B -->

**Variant B — Non-empirical papers (perspective, design science, theoretical, review):**
- Are all claims grounded in cited evidence or explicit reasoning?
- Are warrants (why the evidence supports the conclusion) stated explicitly?
- Are the strongest counter-arguments addressed, not ignored or strawmanned?
- Is the confidence language calibrated to evidence strength? (No "demonstrates" for speculative claims)
- Are boundary conditions stated for all propositions and recommendations?

### 2. Terminology Compliance
- Check adherence to relevant standards (Toulmin argumentation terminology, EQUATOR/reporting guideline terminology, SE vocabulary used consistently)
- Flag non-standard or ambiguous terms
- Verify consistent usage throughout (e.g., "verification" vs "validation", "claim" vs "assertion")

### 3. Structure and Clarity
- Is the logical flow clear?
- Are subsections balanced in length and depth?
- Is there redundancy between subsections?
- Are transitions smooth?
- Does each section's introduction set up what follows?

### 4. Journal Style
- Professional academic tone appropriate for Learned Publishing's scholarly communication audience
- No inappropriate emphasis (bold/italic for generic terms)
- Flowing prose, not telegraphic bullet-point style
- Appropriate level of formality for a Perspective piece
- Accessible to non-specialist readers in publishing and library science

### 5. Completeness
- Are all essential arguments in the main text?
- Is the narrative self-contained for a Perspective paper (3,000–5,000 words)?
- Does the paper make its case without requiring the reader to consult the framework repository?

### 6. Technical Accuracy

<!-- Non-empirical paper — use Variant B -->

**Variant B — Non-empirical papers:**
- Are all key constructs defined (not assumed)?
- Are cited frameworks and models accurately represented (Toulmin, Whetten, Gregor, EQUATOR)?
- Is the relationship between constructs clearly stated?

### 7. Evidence-Language Alignment
- Does hedging language match evidence strength?
- Are speculative claims properly flagged?
- Is own unpublished work (retrofit audits) distinguished from established findings?
- Are the "preliminary evidence" framing markers consistent throughout Section 4?

### 8. Argument Quality (Non-empirical papers only)
- **Toulmin completeness:** Does each major argument have identifiable grounds, warrant, qualifier, and rebuttal?
- **Warrant validity:** Are the inferential bridges appropriate for the target audience (scholarly communication researchers, librarians, publishers)? Would a skeptical expert accept them?
- **Counter-argument engagement:** Are the strongest objections addressed substantively (not dismissed)?
- **Logical coherence:** Do the arguments build on each other without circular reasoning?
- **Contribution clarity:** Is it clear what is new — and what is not — relative to existing literature?
