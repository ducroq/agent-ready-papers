# Whetten 1989

## Bibliographic Info
- **Authors:** David A. Whetten
- **Year:** 1989
- **Title:** What Constitutes a Theoretical Contribution?
- **Venue:** Academy of Management Review, 14(4), 490-495
- **DOI:** 10.5465/amr.1989.4308371

## Summary
Identifies the essential building blocks of a theoretical contribution: What (factors/constructs), How (relationships), Why (underlying logic/warrants), and Who/Where/When (boundary conditions). A theoretical contribution must change our understanding of at least one of these.

## Key Findings
- Four building blocks: What, How, Why, Who/Where/When
- "Why" (the logical reasoning connecting constructs to outcomes) is the most critical and most often missing
- Theory is the EXPLANATION -- not references, data, variables, diagrams, or hypotheses (per Sutton & Staw 1995)
- Boundary conditions (where the theory applies) are essential but often unstated

## Relevance to DR-004
The "Why" component maps directly to Toulmin's warrant. A theoretical paper's core contribution is not a claim that can be source-checked -- it's a logical argument (warrant) connecting constructs. Verification must shift from "does a source say this?" to "is the reasoning valid and are the premises true?" The What/How/Why/When framework provides a natural structure for a "proposition registry" as an alternative to the claim registry.
